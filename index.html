Strategy Development Philosophy & Approach

When an idea for a strategy comes to me, whether from reading a paper, seeing it in forum, hearing it on a podcast, reading it in a book, seeing it on YouTube, my first though is, "That sounds like an interesting coding challenge!"
While I'm coding, I get a deeper understanding of what market weakness is being exploited. This then leads me to be aware of the ways in-which it could easily become over-fit. Which leads me to parameter selection.
If optimization is necessary, I will use RealTest to show me the range of parameters in Trade Count, Net Profit, ROR, MaxDD, MAR, Expectancy. Then I will attempt to use the robust middle of the flattest range.

This programmed discretion is very much a form of art but the decision's are made from what was seen in the backtest, not from guessing.
Doctors don't perform surgery before studying all the data.

Risk Management & Position Sizing

For me, risk management starts with the question, "What instrument is being traded?"
Cooperate risk is no joke. I don't hold any single stocks and I don't use strategies that trade single stocks.
I base my positions using the portfolio's closed-trade-equity x risk amount / ATR(5) and total position size does not exceed 5% of the overall portfolio.
VIX spikes are also considered when opening new positions.
Position sizes are calculated the same for every system.

Some systems have stops in the market, while others do not.
Orders are generated daily, after the market close, and resting orders are placed with the broker.

Tools and Data

I started trading in 2021 using a piece of paper with a list of stocks.
Quickly moved to Excel/Google Sheets and free data from yahoo in 2022.
In fall 2023, Marsten Parker appeared on Mike Covel's podcast. I checked out RealTest and signed up in October along with Norgate Data.
The great thing about RealTest and Norgate is speed!
I can formulate and test a system in RealTest before I could even load and clean Yahoo data in Excel.
I've built a few systems in Python but, honestly, RealTest has me spoiled for system development and daily order generation.
